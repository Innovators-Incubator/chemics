Gas properties module¶

This module contains functions for calculating gas properties such as density, pressure, thermal conductivity, and viscosity. Descriptions of the available gas property functions in the chemics package are provided below.

Density and pressure¶

Functions for estimating gas density and pressure at altitude.

chemics.rhog(mw, Pgas, Tgas)[source]¶

Calculate gas density from molecular weight, pressure, and temperature.

\[\rho = \frac{P * MW}{R * T}\]
Parameters:
  • mw (float) – Molecular weight of gas [g/mol]
  • Pgas (float) – Pressure of the gas [Pa]
  • Tgas (float) – Temperature of the gas [K]
Returns:

rho (float) – Density of gas [kg/m^3]

Examples

>>> rhog(28, 101325, 773)
0.4414
chemics.patm(alt)[source]¶

Determine atmospheric pressure at altitudes up to 51 km. Based on article by Roland Stull [1].

Parameters:alt (float) – Altitude or elevation above sea level [m]
Returns:Patm (float) – Atmospheric pressure [Pa]

References

[1]Practical Meteorology: An Algebra-based Survey of Atmospheric Science by Roland Stull.

Thermal conductivity¶

Functions for determining thermal conductivity of a gas at temperature.

chemics.k_n2(T)[source]¶

Equation for thermal conductivity of nitrogen gas, N2, as a function of temperature based on formula from Yaw’s online reference [2]. Valid at temperatures from 63-1500 K.

\[k_{N_2} = A + B*T + C*T^2 + D*T^3\]
Parameters:T (float) – Temperature of N2 gas [K]
Returns:k (float) – Thermal conductivity of N2 gas [W/mK]

Examples

>>> k_n2(773)
0.0535

References

[2]Yaws’ Transport Properties of Chemicals and Hydrocarbons.

Viscosity¶

Functions for determing viscosity of a gas at temperature.

chemics.mu_h2(T)[source]¶

Calculate viscosity of hydrogen gas, H2, as a function of temperture. Valid at temperatures from 15 K to 1500 K. Equation and coefficients from Yaws’ Handbook [4].

\[\mu_{H_2} = A + B \cdot T + C \cdot T^2 + D \cdot T^3\]
Parameters:T (float) – Temperature of hydrogen gas [K]
Returns:mu (float) – Viscosity of hydrogen gas in micropoise [uP]

Examples

# in units of micropoise, uP
>>> mu_h2(773)
179.73
# in units of kilogram per meter per second, kg/(m s)
>>> mu_h2(773) / 1e7
1.79e-05

References

[4]Yaws’ Handbook of Properties of the Chemical Elements.

\[\mu_{N_2} = A + B \cdot T + C \cdot T^2 + D \cdot T^3\]
Parameters:T (float) – Tempreature of nitrogen gas [K]
Returns:mug (float) – Viscosity of nitrogen gas in micropoise [uP]

Examples
